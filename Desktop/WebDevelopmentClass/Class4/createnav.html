City of Brotherly Love

The history of Philadelphia all began in 1682, when the city was founded by William Penn. This city includes historic monuments such as the Liberty Bell, Independence Hall, the Betsy Ross House, and the National Constitution Center.

Philadelphia is a sports city through and through. Philadelphia is one of twelve cities that hosts teams in all four major sports leagues in North America, and Philadelphia is one of just three cities in which one team from every league plays within city limits. The four major teams include the Philadelphia Phillies, Philadelphia Eagles, Philadelphia Flyers, and Philadelphia 76ers.

South Philadelphia

South Philadelphia is home to some of the best cheesesteaks in the country and to the Philadelphia sports complex. Current facilities in the area include:

  • The Wells Fargo Center (formerly the CoreStates Center, the First Union Center and the Wachovia Center) opened in 1996 and is home to the Philadelphia Flyers (NHL), Philadelphia 76ers (NBA), Philadelphia Wings (NLL), and Philadelphia Soul (AFL).

  • Lincoln Financial Field (known as "The Linc") opened in 2003 and is home to the Philadelphia Eagles (NFL) and Temple Owls (NCAA football).

  • Citizens Bank Park opened in 2004 and is home to the Philadelphia Phillies (MLB).
